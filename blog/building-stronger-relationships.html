Building Stronger Relationships

Home/Blog/Building Stronger Relationships
June 15, 2026 Community Reverend Samuel Ojediran

Relationships are the fabric of human existence. From the very beginning, God declared that it is not good for man to be alone. He designed us for connection, fellowship, and community. Yet in our modern world, relationships are often strained, shallow, or broken. How do we build relationships that last?

The Foundation: Love

The Apostle Paul gives us a clear roadmap in 1 Corinthians 13. Love is patient, kind, does not envy, does not boast, and is not proud. Before we expect others to treat us well, we must first cultivate a heart of genuine love. Love is the foundation upon which every strong relationship is built.

Love is not merely a feeling; it is a choice and a commitment. When you choose to love someone, you commit to their well-being even when they are not at their best. This is the kind of love that transforms marriages, friendships, and families.

Communication Is Key

Many relationships break down not because of a lack of love, but because of a lack of healthy communication. James 1:19 tells us to be quick to listen, slow to speak, and slow to become angry. Active listening is a powerful tool. When you truly listen to understand rather than to respond, you build trust and intimacy.

Learn to express your feelings honestly but respectfully. Use "I" statements instead of "you" accusations. Instead of "You never listen," try "I feel unheard when..." This simple shift can transform the atmosphere of any conversation.

Forgiveness Heals

No relationship is perfect because no person is perfect. Conflict is inevitable, but bitterness is optional. Ephesians 4:32 tells us to be kind and compassionate, forgiving one another just as Christ forgave us. Forgiveness does not excuse the offense; it frees you from its hold on your heart.

Invest Time

Strong relationships require time and intentionality. Schedule regular time with your spouse, your children, and your friends. Quality time communicates value. In a distracted world, the gift of your full attention is priceless.

"A friend loves at all times, and a brother is born for a time of adversity." — Proverbs 17:17
